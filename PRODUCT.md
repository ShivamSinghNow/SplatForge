# Product

## Register

product

## Users

SplatForge is for robotics builders, embodied AI researchers, hackathon judges, and technical operators who need to see whether a robot policy improves after practicing in reconstructed 3D worlds.

## Product Purpose

SplatForge runs a recursive self-improvement loop: reconstruct a world, attempt a robot task, critique failure with an AI council, generate harder practice worlds, train an adapter, and retest the original failure to prove improvement.

## Brand Personality

Serious, technical, ambitious. The interface should feel like a robotics lab control room, not a generic SaaS admin panel.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing dashboards, colorful template cards, generic workspace navigation, toy demos, chat-wrapper layouts, and settings-first information architecture. Integrations are system health, not the product story.

## Design Principles

- Show the loop: robot fails, AI council critiques, curriculum is generated, policy trains, retest proves improvement.
- Make the reconstructed world the product center, with controls and evidence supporting it.
- Preserve system scope while reducing confusion through clear labels and progressive disclosure.
- Treat demo data as explicit fixtures, never as scattered UI constants.
- Prioritize readability under pressure: high contrast, direct labels, clear state, and obvious next actions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast, readable font sizes, keyboard-focusable controls, visible loading/error states, and reduced-motion friendly transitions.
